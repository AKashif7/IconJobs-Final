Our Story

Built to make finding work simple again.

IconJobs was created because existing job platforms were failing people — ghost listings, zero feedback, confusing interfaces, and no focus on short-term local work.

Our Mission

Quality connections between real people, with zero friction.

IconJobs was built on a simple belief: finding short-term local work — or finding the right person for it — shouldn't involve agents, subscriptions, or three-week waits. Too many job boards are cluttered with noise, outdated listings, and zero transparency between employers and applicants.

We focus on quality over quantity. Every feature on IconJobs is designed to help job seekers present themselves confidently, and to help employers post roles that attract the right candidates, not just the most.

Platform Breakdown

Indeed General Board

Indeed is the world's largest job aggregator, excellent for volume — but poorly suited for micro or casual work. Listings are often aggregated from third-party sites and go stale without warning. There is no real-time communication, no application status feedback for seekers, and no focus on local in-person roles. For short-term gig work, Indeed is essentially a dead end.

LinkedIn Professional Network

LinkedIn is the gold standard for permanent professional roles but is a poor fit for micro or casual jobs. Key features — such as messaging non-connections and seeing who viewed your application — are locked behind premium subscriptions costing upwards of £30/month. Its interface is designed for career networking, not rapid local hiring.

Gumtree Classifieds

Gumtree does list casual and part-time roles, but it is fundamentally a classifieds site, not a job platform. There is no application tracking, no messaging system, and contact details — including phone numbers — are often publicly visible from the moment a listing goes live, creating serious privacy and safety concerns for job seekers.

TaskRabbit / Fiverr Service Marketplaces

TaskRabbit focuses exclusively on home and handyman services (cleaning, furniture assembly, DIY). Fiverr caters to remote freelancers globally. Neither platform supports the kind of general short-term local employment — warehouse work, event staffing, kitchen porter shifts — that IconJobs is built for. Both also charge significant commissions of up to 20–25% per transaction.

How It Started

From a university brief to a real platform.

IconJobs began as a final-year computer science project at the University of Westminster with a clear brief: build a job board that genuinely helps people. The frustration with existing platforms — ghost jobs, ignored applications, zero feedback loops, and no focus on the kind of short-term, in-person, paid work that many Londoners rely on — became the foundation for a cleaner, more human approach to micro-recruitment.

The platform was designed from the ground up with privacy and trust at its core. Contact details are never publicly exposed. Every account holder must declare that their profile is 100% authentic and that false information may result in referral to UK law enforcement — a standard no major job board currently enforces. This keeps the quality of the community high.

Today IconJobs offers real-time job listings, direct messaging between candidates and employers, profile ratings, application status tracking, and tools to help both sides put their best foot forward. The goal has always been simple: get the right people connected as fast as possible, with as little friction as possible.
