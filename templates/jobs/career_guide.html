Career Guide

Everything you need to find your next role — or hire your next great person.

How to Use IconJobs

1. Create Your Account

Click Join Free and choose the Job Seeker role. Fill in your first name, last name, and a strong password. Once registered, go to Edit Profile to add your bio, skills, location, and a professional photo — employers read your profile before they read your CV.

2. Find the Right Jobs

Use Browse Jobs and apply filters — keyword, location, job type, and category — to narrow results. Hit the bookmark icon on any listing to save it for later. Check My Applications from your dashboard to track every role you've applied to and its current status.

3. Apply with Confidence

On the job detail page click Apply Now. Write a tailored cover letter (see CV guide below), attach your CV, and submit. You can message the employer directly via Messages to ask questions before or after applying.

Writing Your Best CV

A generic CV gets ignored. Read the job description carefully and mirror the language and keywords the employer uses. If they say "stakeholder management", use exactly those words — many companies use automated screening tools that scan for keyword matches before a human ever reads your CV.

  • Personal Statement (3–4 lines) — who you are, what you bring, what you're looking for.
  • Work Experience — most recent first. Use bullet points starting with action verbs (Led, Built, Reduced, Delivered). Quantify wherever you can: "Reduced onboarding time by 30%".
  • Education — degree, institution, year. Include relevant modules only.
  • Skills — hard skills (tools, languages, certifications) and 2–3 soft skills backed by evidence.
  • References — "Available on request" is fine to save space.

Keep it to one page (two pages maximum for senior roles). Use a clean, single-column layout with consistent fonts — avoid tables and columns as they can break applicant tracking systems (ATS). Save and send as PDF so formatting is preserved on every device. Name the file FirstName_LastName_CV.pdf.

Your cover letter answers "Why this role at this company?". Open with a specific hook — reference a recent company achievement or explain exactly why their mission excites you. In the middle paragraph, connect two or three of your experiences directly to the job requirements. Close by confirming availability and inviting a conversation. Keep it under 300 words.

  • Spelling or grammar errors — always proofread and use a spell-checker.
  • Generic objectives like "seeking a challenging role" — be specific.
  • Including a photo (in the UK and many other countries this can introduce unconscious bias).
  • Listing responsibilities without achievements — show impact, not just duties.
  • Using a personal email like coolboy1999@hotmail.com — create a professional one.

How to Post a Job on IconJobs

1. Set Up Your Employer Profile

Register with the Employer role and complete your profile. Add your company name, a professional bio, and a logo or photo. Candidates research companies before applying — a complete profile increases application rates significantly.

2. Create Your Job Post

Click + Post a Job in the navigation bar. Fill in every field — title, category, location, salary range, duration, and a detailed description. The more complete your post, the higher it ranks in search results and the better-matched your applicants will be.

3. Manage Applications

Review all applicants from My Jobs on your dashboard. Update each application status (Under Review, Shortlisted, Rejected, Offered) to keep candidates informed. Use Messages to communicate directly with promising candidates and arrange interviews.

Writing the Perfect Job Post

Job seekers search by title. Use the standard industry term candidates are likely to type, e.g. "Software Engineer" not "Code Ninja". Include level where relevant: "Junior Frontend Developer" or "Senior Data Analyst". Avoid internal jargon, abbreviations, and all-caps titles.

A complete job post includes:
  • Salary or salary range — posts with salary get up to 3× more applicants. If there's a range, state it honestly.
  • Location and remote policy — on-site, hybrid, or fully remote, and the office location.
  • Contract type and duration — full-time, part-time, fixed-term, freelance.
  • Start date — even an approximate date helps candidates plan.
  • Key responsibilities — 5–8 bullet points of what the person will actually do day-to-day.
  • Requirements — split into essential and desirable. Keep the essential list short — every extra requirement reduces your applicant pool.
  • What you offer — benefits, culture, team size, growth opportunities.

Write in second person ("You will lead..." not "The candidate will lead..."). Be direct and warm. Avoid excessive corporate language. Check for gender-coded words — terms like "rockstar", "aggressive", or "dominant" can deter women and other candidates. Use inclusive language throughout.

Choose the correct Category so your post appears in filtered searches. Include relevant keywords naturally in the description — tools, technologies, industry terms. Posts with complete descriptions and salary information are ranked higher in search results on IconJobs.

Top candidates are often off the market within days. Aim to review applications within 48 hours and keep every applicant informed of their status — even a rejection message protects your employer brand. Use the Messages feature to move fast with strong candidates.
